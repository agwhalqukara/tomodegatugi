The 21 Best Sweatshirts for Women of 2024

 Posted on August 10, 2024  |  17 minutes  |  3572 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Sweatshirts are more versatile than you might think, and can look just as stylish as they feel cozy — it’s just a question of what you’re going for. There’s so much variety among sweatshirts — from fitted to oversized designs and hoodies to crewnecks  — that we figured you could use a little help navigating what’s on offer. We did some extensive research and spoke to several style experts to help you find the best of the sweatshirt world, whether you’re after a matching sweatsuit, a lightweight layer, or a fuzzy hoodie to bundle up in.

Our faves come in a whole lot of color options, with inclusive sizing, different lengths available, and top-notch sustainability credentials. These are the sweatshirts you’ll be layering all fall, winter, and beyond.

What to Keep in Mind

  • Fit: Sweatshirts come in variations of fitted, relaxed, and oversized, and which type of fit you go for is really up to you. As a general rule, you should balance out your silhouette, so a more oversized style will look fab over some leggings or bike shorts, while a more fitted design is great over a pair of jeans. At the end of the day, it just depends on what you’re more comfortable with. For those that are on the ends of the height spectrum, look for designs that come in either tall or petite sizes, from brands  Gap, Madewell, and Anthropologie.
  • Materials: Choosing the materials for a sweatshirt depends on your personal preferences, as well as what seasons you intend to wear your sweatshirt in and for which occasions. For example, a lightweight crew neck is great for an autumnal Starbucks run, while a velour hoodie makes the best companion for hibernating in the dead of winter. “If you’re looking for warmth, go with fleece,” says fashion blogger and stylist AK Brown. If you plan to work out in your sweatshirt, look for a hoodie with breathable cotton and just a touch of stretch.
  • Functionality: Just like with the materials, you want to consider when you'll be wearing your sweatshirt and how functional you need it to be. While a basic crew neck might be the right choice for lounging around, a design with special features like pockets, an adjustable waist, and a hood is a better fit when exercising or running errands.

Your Questions, Answered

What's the difference between a sweatshirt and hoodie?

“Sweatshirt” is a catch-all for a sweater that’s not knitted or woven (though knitted and woven sweatshirts also exist). Within the category, hoodies come with hoods while crewnecks have a simple, round neckline. There are also zipped hoodies, half-zips, and pullovers, which come with their own advantages. While pullovers are effortless, some prefer having the flexibility of closing and opening a zip-up. Bonus tip: Zip up hoodies are great to wear when getting ready since they won't mess up your hair or makeup.

How do you style a sweatshirt to look more like a model off duty?

The most recognizable supermodels of our time have done us a huge favor: They’ve elevated the humble sweatshirt into a must-have piece for looking effortlessly cool and like you’re expecting to be papped on your way home from yoga. “Take it from Kendall Jenner, Kristin Cavallari, and J.Lo: Make sure your sweatshirt fits you like a glove and pair it with leggings,” Sanchez suggests. “Also look for some cute footwear to make you go that extra stylish mile.” We love this combo with statement sneakers or slides.

Stewart offers another variation on this uniform. “If you want to go full model, the best way to style your sweatshirt is to buy it truly oversized and wear it more like a dress with biker shorts,” she says. “It's a true Princess Diana moment coming full circle.” Last but not least, if you’re aiming for more street style and less athleisure, “sweatshirts over oversized white button-down shirts are always a hit,” Brown says. This look is “very elevated and layered but only two pieces of clothing are needed.”
