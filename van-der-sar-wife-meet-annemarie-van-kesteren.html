Who is Van Der Sar

Van der Sar, a Voorhout native, started his professional career at Foreholte before moving on to VV Noordwijk. He was introduced to Louis van Gaal at a somewhat late age, and he later signed for Ajax. Despite arriving late, he joined the reserve squad and bided his time before Louis van Gaal called him up to the first team.

He had a long and fruitful run with their senior team, winning the 1991–92 UEFA Cup and the 1994–95 UEFA Champions League (by this point the starting goalkeeper), as well as the 1995 Best European Goalkeeper award, in addition to domestic accolades.

Van der Sar is regarded as one of the greatest goalkeepers of all time by critics and other athletes. He is also one of the greatest players to ever play, with a career total of 26 major trophies, mostly attained while playing for Ajax and Manchester United.

He established a record by going 1,311 minutes without giving up a league goal in the 2008–09 season. He was 40 years and 205 days old when he won the Premier League in 2011, making him the oldest player to accomplish it. Van der Sar has received numerous individual honours, including UEFA Club Goalkeeper of the Year in 2009 and Best European Goalkeeper in 1995 and 2009.

He played in 226 games for Ajax in all, and in the 1997–98 season, he converted a penalty to give Ajax an 8–1 win over De Graafschap. He earned the Dutch Football Goalkeeper of the Year award in his first full season as Ajax No. 1 and went on to hold the title for the following three years.
