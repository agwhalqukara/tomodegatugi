Top Facts about Dwight Frye

  • Dwight Iliff Frye (born Fry) was an American character actor of stage and screen.
  • He is best known for his portrayals of neurotic, murderous villains in classic Universal horror films.
  • He studied for a career in music and first appeared as a concert pianist.
  • In 1931, he played Renfield in “Dracula” and Fritz in “Frankenstein”.
  • He specialized in playing mentally unbalanced characters.
  • In 1933, he appeared in “The Invisible Man” and in 1935 in “The Crime of Dr. Crespi”.
  • In 1935, he appeared in “Bride of Frankenstein”.
  • During the early 1940s, Frye alternated between film roles and appearing on stage.
  • In 1943, he died of a heart attack in Hollywood.
  • He is interred in Glendale’s Forest Lawn Memorial Park.
  • Alice Cooper wrote a tribute track to Dwight Frye entitled “The Ballad of Dwight Fry”.
  • Devil Doll’s 1990 album, Eliogabalus, features the photographed likeness of Dwight Frye on one of its booths.
  • SNFU’s 1991 compilation album The Last of the Big Time Suspenders features artwork of Dwight Frye as his “Renfield” character on the cover.
  • He made a contribution to the war effort by working nights as a tool designer for Lockheed Aircraft.
  • He had a few minor comedic roles in silent pictures with the coming of sound.
